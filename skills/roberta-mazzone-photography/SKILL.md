# Roberta Mazzone Photography

## Quick Start

Use this skill to translate a brief into slow-travel lifestyle photo direction with an architectural eye. Favor atmosphere, place, light, proportion, and quiet human presence over generic postcard travel or loud influencer posing.

Read [references/style-notes.md](references/style-notes.md) when the user asks for detailed image prompts, a campaign direction, a shoot plan, or a closer explanation of the visual language.

## Workflow

1. Identify the deliverable:
   - Image-generation prompt
   - Shot list
   - Moodboard direction
   - Travel/editorial story
   - Lifestyle or hospitality campaign brief
   - Caption or short copy
2. Anchor the image in place:
   - Use a specific city, coast, room, street, balcony, hotel, courtyard, market, studio, or transit moment.
   - Include architecture or design as a compositional structure, not just background decoration.
3. Shape the mood:
   - Slow, sunlit, composed, cinematic, intimate, restrained, tactile.
   - Let daily rituals carry the story: arrival, morning light, walking, resting, packing, reading, coffee, linen, shadow, water, stone, ceramics.
4. Build the frame:
   - Prioritize negative space, clean geometry, thresholds, arches, windows, stair lines, tiled floors, textured walls, and shadow patterns.
   - Use one human figure or a small gesture when needed; keep it natural and unperformed.
5. Tune the palette and light:
   - Prefer warm natural light, softened contrast, sun-washed neutrals, Mediterranean blues, terracotta, pale stone, off-white linen, muted greens, and wood.
   - Avoid over-saturation, heavy HDR, fake vintage filters, loud orange-teal grading, or decorative bokeh.
6. Deliver in the user's requested language and format.

## Prompt Pattern

For image prompts, include these fields when useful:

```text
Subject and place:
Composition:
Light:
Palette and texture:
Camera feel:
Mood:
Avoid:
```

Example:

```text
Subject and place: a quiet Sicilian guesthouse balcony after breakfast, one linen chair, folded newspaper, ceramic cup, sea light in the distance
Composition: architectural framing through a doorway, strong verticals, negative space, low table in the lower third
Light: late morning natural light, soft wall shadows, gentle highlights on stone and fabric
Palette and texture: off-white linen, pale limestone, muted blue, aged wood, hand-painted ceramic
Camera feel: documentary lifestyle photograph, 35mm equivalent, natural perspective, restrained depth of field
Mood: slow travel, cinematic stillness, refined but lived-in
Avoid: tourist crowd, staged influencer pose, glossy luxury hotel look, oversaturated colors, fake film scratches
```

## Guardrails

- Treat the result as Roberta Mazzone-inspired direction, not an exact copy of a living creator's work.
- Do not claim private access to Instagram content; use the bundled notes as a public-source style summary and browse again if the user asks for current bio, metrics, or recent posts.
- Avoid using Roberta Mazzone as a depicted person unless the user explicitly asks for biographical or profile writing.
- Keep luxury subtle. The visual should feel cultivated and lived-in, not like a generic premium travel advertisement.
- If details are missing, make reasonable assumptions and state them briefly only when they affect the creative direction.
