# Style Notes

These notes summarize public-source cues for Roberta Mazzone / @_lagiuditta and convert them into reusable creative direction. They are not a full scrape of Instagram.

## Public Identity Cues

- PhotoVogue lists Roberta Mazzone in Francofonte, Italy, links to `instagram.com/_lagiuditta/`, and categorizes the work around fashion, landscape, architecture, advertising, and indoors.
- Psicografici Editore describes her as Sicilian, trained in building engineering and architecture at the University of Catania, a traveler and photographer who bought her first DSLR in 2014, and a lifestyle content creator connected to communication, digital work, architecture, and design aesthetics.
- The Lane describes her as a slow-travel photographer with an architectural eye, a nomadic spirit, a Fuji camera, quiet precision, cinematic instinct, and framing shaped by light, architecture, and restraint.

## Visual Grammar

- Architectural eye: use thresholds, facades, arches, windows, balconies, staircases, colonnades, tiled surfaces, room corners, and strong geometric lines.
- Slow travel: show presence through unhurried gestures, traces of use, local textures, and small rituals instead of landmark collecting.
- Mediterranean restraint: favor sun, stone, linen, water, muted blues, terracotta, cream, olive, shadow, aged wood, ceramic, and plaster.
- Cinematic stillness: compose like a paused scene, with a clear foreground, middle ground, and background when possible.
- Lifestyle realism: include evidence of a person without forcing a pose: a hand on a railing, sandals near a bed, a half-packed suitcase, a hat on a chair, a table after breakfast.
- Design sensitivity: make objects feel selected and spatially coherent; avoid clutter unless it is narratively useful.

## Useful Shot Types

- Doorway or window looking into a room with travel traces.
- Balcony scene with linen, ceramic cup, book, shadow, and distant water or rooftops.
- Street corner or courtyard where architecture frames one small human figure.
- Hotel room detail with natural fabric, morning light, suitcase, and local object.
- Coastal or island landscape with restrained human scale and a strong horizon.
- Market, cafe, or table scene where food and objects are quiet supporting details.
- Product-in-place shot where the product belongs to the travel ritual instead of dominating the frame.

## Copy Tone

Use copy that is concise, sensory, and place-led. Prefer concrete nouns and verbs. Avoid breathless luxury language, empty wanderlust phrases, and over-explaining the mood.

Good directions:

- "Morning light crossing limestone and linen."
- "A quiet arrival, held by blue shadow and warm stone."
- "Design, travel, and ordinary rituals in one composed frame."

Avoid:

- "Ultimate dream escape."
- "Luxury vibes."
- "Instagrammable hidden gem."
- "Wanderlust magic."

## Negative Prompt Ideas
